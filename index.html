My choice regarding the Chatbot tool

Glauco Labone - May 22, 2020

I have always been interested in subjects related to the interaction between user and machine and the benefits that this can bring in our lives. On conversations with colleagues from specialized websites, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are terms that are always present on most job openings.

Putting these interesting topics together, I started to study chatbot. It is a computational intelligence used to conduct an online conversation via text or text to speech between machine and human, meaning that, when one needs help or information on a certain topic, this artificial intelligence is able to provide the necessary guidelines. Despite being a much-discussed technology nowadays, the first experiments were made in 1964 and since then, this technology has been increasingly explored and applied in different areas.

Incredible, don’t you think? The first thing that comes to mind is the possibility of reducing companies costs and the average service time (e.g. call centre services). That would allow - as a consequence - higher productivity with lower costs. herefore, I tried to understand more what are the companies that stand out in this sector and what are the most popular tools so I could choose which one I will specialize in…

I did some research and learned more about IBM Watson, which is, without a doubt a very interesting and complete tool. I really liked the possibility of customizing the user interface according to the needs of your business and the out-of-the-box connectivity to SharePoint Online and Salesforce. SAP has a very powerful tool, SAP Conversational AI. Like IBM Watson, SAP Conversational AI has Integration with SAP and non-SAP solutions. Google has a tool much-used nowadays, the famous DialogFlow. I highlight the success case of Domino’s, being one of the pioneer pizzerias to use a bot for ordering pizzas. I should also mention Microsoft with its easy-to-use Azure Bot Service tool which includes workshops like Chatbot-in-a-Day.

All of these tools will undoubtedly deliver extraordinary results for companies and help drive the digital transformation through a quick return on investment, but I still wasn’t decided on my choice until I came across AWS Lex, the Amazon’s tool. It is complete, intuitive, and very robust.

According to information on the Amazon website itself, Amazon Lex is a service for building conversational interfaces using voice and text. With Lex, the same deep learning engine that powers Alexa is now available to any developer, enabling you to bring sophisticated, natural language chatbots to your new and existing applications. Amazon Lex enables any developer to build conversational chatbots quickly and easily. With Amazon Lex, no deep learning expertise is necessary. You just specify the basic conversational flow directly from the console, and then Amazon Lex manages the dialogue and dynamically adjusts the response.

My first contact with this service was very interesting, as it is not necessary to install any application on the computer and it is a great advantage. Everything you need to do to create your bot and have an AWS account and access the AWS Management Console.

There is an option to create your bot using a template or starting from scratch. If you are not familiar with Lex, I recommend you choose one of the three models available: booking trips, ordering flowers, or making appointments.

In my next posts, I will explain the step by step for creating your first Amazon Lex bot and will go into detail about possible parameters and concepts like "sample utterances", "slots", "confirmation prompt", "fulfillment" and "response". Also, I will explain the possibility of using a very valuable computing service called AWS Lambda to interact with external interfaces.
