My choice about the Chatbot tool

Glauco Labone - May 22, 2020

I have always been interested in subjects related to the interaction between user versus machine and the facilities that this can provide in our lives. In conversations with friends who work with technology, on specialized websites, in most job openings, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are words that are always present.

Putting these interesting topics together, I started to study chatbot. It is a computational intelligence used to conduct an online conversation via text or text to speech between machine and human, that is, you need help or information, and at least in the first interests, this computational intelligence through a robot will provide you the necessary guidelines. Despite being a much-discussed technology nowadays, the first experiments were made in 1994 and since then, this technology has been increasingly explored and powered in more resources.

Incredible, don’t you think? It has already come to my mind to reduce costs for companies, to reduce the average service time by thinking of a call center… the dream is that any executive who thinks about productivity with lean costs. Therefore, I tried to understand more who are the companies that stand out in this branch and what are the most popular tools for me to choose which one I will specialize in…

I started and understood more about IBM Watson, without a doubt very interesting and complete. I really liked the possibility of customizing the user interface according to the needs of your business and the out-of-the-box connectivity to SharePoint Online and Salesforce. SAP has a very powerful tool, SAP Conversational AI. Like IBM Watson, SAP Conversational AI has Integration with SAP and non-SAP solutions. Google has a tool much-used nowadays, the famous DialogFlow. I highlight the success case of Domino’s, being one of the pioneer pizzerias to use a bot for ordering pizzas. I can’t help mentioning Microsoft with its easy-to-use Azure Bot Service tool to the point where the company provides workshops like Chatbot-in-a-Day.

All of these tools will undoubtedly deliver extraordinary results for companies and help drive the digital transformation through a quick return on investment, but I still wasn’t decided on my choice until I came across AWS Lex, the Amazon’s tool. It is complete, intuitive, and very robust.

According to information on the Amazon website itself, Amazon Lex is a service for building conversational interfaces using voice and text. With Lex, the same deep learning engine that powers Alexa is now available to any developer, enabling you to bring sophisticated, natural language chatbots to your new and existing applications. Amazon Lex enables any developer to build conversational chatbots quickly and easily. With Amazon Lex, no deep learning expertise is necessary. You just specify the basic conversational flow directly from the console, and then Amazon Lex manages the dialogue and dynamically adjusts the response.

My first contact with this service was very interesting, as it is not necessary to install any application on the computer and it is a great advantage. Everything you need to do to create your bot and have an AWS account and access the AWS Management Console.

There is an option to create your bot using a template or not. If you are not familiar with Lex, I recommend you choose one of the three models available: booking trips, ordering flowers, or making appointments.

From the next posts, I will explain the step by step for creating your first Amazon Lex bot and will go into detail about possible parameters and concepts like "sample utterances", "slots", "confirmation prompt", "fulfillment" and "response". Also, I will explain the possibility of using a very valuable computing service called AWS Lambda to interact with external interfaces.
